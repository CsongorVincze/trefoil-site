Open and closed sets

👨‍🏫: Now that we have closure let’s define open and closed sets.

Definition: Open and Closed Sets

Let A⊆RpA \subseteq \mathbb{R}^{p}A⊆Rp.

1. AAA is open, if every point of AAA is an interior point:

∀x∈A:x∈int⁡A\forall x \in A : x \in \operatorname{int} A∀x∈A:x∈intA

2. AAA is closed, if its complement is open:

Ac is openA^{c} \text{ is open}Ac is open

Two equivalent characterizations of closed sets:

  • If AAA is closed, then ∂A⊆A\partial A \subseteq A∂A⊆A
  • A‾\overline{A}A is always a closed set

Note: Not every set is open or closed. The easiest counterexample is [0,1[[0, 1[[0,1[.

👨‍🏫: Let’s unpack open sets a bit. Intuitively it means that however close we are to the boundary, we can always “draw” a sphere with radius ε>0\varepsilon > 0ε>0 that is entirely inside AAA.

Properties of open and closed sets

👨‍🏫: We have two important statements for open sets and their pairs for closed sets.

Properties of Open and Closed Sets

Open sets:

(a) The union of arbitrarily many (even infinitely many) open sets is open.

(b) The intersection of finitely many open sets is open.

Closed sets:

(c) The intersection of arbitrarily many (even infinitely many) closed sets is closed.

(d) The union of finitely many closed sets is closed.
