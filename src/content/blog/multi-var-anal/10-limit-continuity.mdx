## Limit Continuity

Let's look define limit and continuity for multivariable functions. Actually it will be really similar to what we did in single variable functions.

```mdx
<AxiomBox lang="en" title="Definition: Limit of Multivariable Function">
Let f: \mathbb{R}^{p} \to \mathbb{R} let x, x_0 \in \mathbb{R}^{p}


</AxiomBox>
```


{/* 
Let $f: D_f \subseteq \mathbb{R}^{p} \to \mathbb{R}$ and let $a$ be an accumulation point of $D_f$. We say that the **limit** of $f$ as $x$ approaches $a$ is $L \in \mathbb{R}$, denoted $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L$, if:

$$
\forall\, \varepsilon > 0 \quad \exists\, \delta > 0 : \forall x \in D_f, \quad 0 < \|x - a\| < \delta \implies |f(x) - L| < \varepsilon
$$ */}
